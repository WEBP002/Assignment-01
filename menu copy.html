Grilled asparagus salad with lemon vinaigrette (spring)

description
This refreshing spring salad features grilled asparagus served on a bed of mixed greens and topped with a tangy lemon vinaigrette dressing.
preparation method
Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Toss the asparagus with 1 tbsp of olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Grill the asparagus for 3-4 minutes per side, until tender and lightly charred. In a small bowl, whisk together the remaining olive oil, lemon juice, honey, minced garlic, salt, and pepper to make the dressing. Arrange the mixed greens on a large platter or individual plates. Top the greens with the grilled asparagus. Drizzle the lemon vinaigrette over the salad and serve immediately.
ingredients
  • 1 bunch of asparagus, tough ends trimmed
  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Roasted root vegetables with rosemary (winter)

description
This hearty winter dish features a medley of roasted root vegetables with a touch of rosemary. The vegetables are tender and caramelized, with a subtle earthy flavor that pairs perfectly with the aromatic rosemary.
preparation method
Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large mixing bowl, toss the chopped vegetables, garlic, olive oil, and rosemary together until they are evenly coated. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Spread the vegetable mixture in a single layer on a large baking sheet. Roast in the preheated oven for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and browned. Remove from the oven and serve hot as a side dish.
ingredients
  • 2 medium-sized sweet potatoes, peeled and chopped into small cubes
  • 2 medium-sized parsnips, peeled and chopped into small cubes
  • 2 medium-sized carrots, peeled and chopped into small cubes
  • 1 medium-sized onion, chopped into small pieces
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh rosemary, chopped

Lobster risotto with truffle oil

description
Creamy and decadent, this lobster risotto is infused with truffle oil for an added layer of richness and depth of flavor.
preparation method
In a large saucepan, heat the chicken broth over medium heat. In a separate pan, sauté the shallot and garlic in butter until translucent. Add the Arborio rice to the pan and stir for 1-2 minutes until the rice is coated in the butter mixture. Add the white wine and stir until the wine has been absorbed by the rice. Begin adding the hot chicken broth to the rice mixture, one cup at a time, stirring constantly until each cup has been absorbed before adding the next. Once the rice is cooked to your desired consistency, add the cooked lobster meat, truffle oil, Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper, stirring to combine. Serve immediately, garnished with additional Parmesan cheese and a drizzle of truffle oil if desired.
ingredients
  • 1 lobster, cooked and meat removed
  • 11/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter

Pan-seared duck breast with cherry reduction sauce

description
Pan-seared duck breast with cherry reduction sauce is a delicious and elegant dish that features tender and juicy duck breast complemented by a tangy and slightly sweet cherry reduction sauce.
preparation method
Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Score the skin of the duck breasts in a crisscross pattern, being careful not to cut into the meat. Season the duck breasts generously with salt and black pepper. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the olive oil to the skillet and place the duck breasts skin side down in the skillet. Cook for about 4-5 minutes, until the skin is golden brown and crispy. Flip the duck breasts over and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Transfer the duck breasts to a baking sheet and roast in the oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare. Remove the duck breasts from the oven and let them rest for 5-10 minutes. While the duck breasts are resting, make the cherry reduction sauce. In the same skillet used to cook the duck breasts, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped shallot and garlic, and sauté until softened, about 2-3 minutes. Add the chicken stock, red wine, chopped cherries, honey, and balsamic vinegar to the skillet. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer until it has reduced by half and thickened, about 10-15 minutes. Season the cherry reduction sauce with salt and black pepper to taste. Slice the duck breasts and serve them with the cherry reduction sauce drizzled on top.
ingredients
  • 2 duck breasts, skin on
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 cup chicken stock
